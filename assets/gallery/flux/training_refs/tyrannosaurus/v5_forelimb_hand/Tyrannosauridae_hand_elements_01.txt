Comparative skeletal diagram of tyrannosaurid forelimb manus showing four species: Albertosaurus, Tarbosaurus, Daspletosaurus, and Tyrannosaurus rex. All show two-fingered hand with digits I and II only, vestigial metacarpal III stub not forming a functional finger. Tyrannosaurus rex panel at far right shows thickest arm bones of the group, metacarpal III and digit II unfused, two large recurved laterally compressed claws on digits I and II, no third functional claw. Digit I shorter and stouter, digit II longer with larger distal claw. Scientific reference establishing correct two-digit manus anatomy for Tyrannosaurus
